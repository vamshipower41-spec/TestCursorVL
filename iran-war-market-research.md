# Iran War Market Research — March 22, 2026

## Conflict Summary (Day 23)

The US-Israel military campaign against Iran is in its 4th week, triggered by the assassination of Iran's Supreme Leader Khamenei on February 28, 2026.

### Key Developments
- Strait of Hormuz near-totally blocked — 3,000+ vessels stranded
- Trump issued 48-hour ultimatum: reopen Hormuz or power plants will be targeted
- Iran broke through Israeli defenses, hitting Dimona and Arad (100+ wounded)
- Brent crude at $112.19/barrel — highest of the war
- Trump hinted at "winding down" but deploying more troops
- 1,444 killed in Iran including 204 children

## Market Positives

1. **Defense stocks rallying** — drone, satellite, missile defense companies benefiting
2. **Energy sector** — oil & gas companies profiting from $100+ oil
3. **S&P 500 resilient** — only ~-2% YTD, markets pricing in a short conflict
4. **Historical pattern** — geopolitical volatility tends to be temporary
5. **Ceasefire catalyst** — Trump hinting at "winding down" could trigger relief rally
6. **Gold** performing as safe haven

## Key Risks

- Prolonged Hormuz closure → stagflation risk
- Fed boxed in — can't cut rates with oil-driven inflation
- Goldman Sachs warns high oil prices could last through 2027
- Worst case: asset bubble burst + debt crisis

## Bottom Line

Net negative for markets. Bullish case depends on quick resolution. Defense and energy are the only clear sector winners. Watch for ceasefire signals as the buy catalyst.
